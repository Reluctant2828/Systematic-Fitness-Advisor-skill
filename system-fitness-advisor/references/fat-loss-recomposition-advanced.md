# Fat Loss And Body Recomposition Advanced Knowledge Base

Use this reference with `goal-fat-loss-recomposition.md` when the user asks about 减脂, 塑形, 体脂下降, 变瘦, recomposition, 保肌减脂, 有氧安排, NEAT, 平台期, 腰腹线条, 肩背臀腿比例, or how to adjust training while dieting.

## Core model

减脂塑形 is not only weight loss. It manages body fat, muscle retention, visual proportion, training performance, and recovery.

Core logic:

- Calorie deficit lowers fat.
- Resistance training and protein preserve muscle.
- NEAT and cardio expand energy expenditure.
- Sleep, stress, and periodization determine whether the plan can last.
- Local training shapes muscle and posture, but cannot promise local fat loss.

## Decision algorithm

| Step | Input or judgment | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Height, weight, sex, age, training age, timeline | Classify goal: fat loss, recomposition, body shaping, posture, weight management, contest-style cut. |
| 2 | Current diet, weight trend, activity, training frequency | Estimate maintenance context and current bottleneck. |
| 3 | Body-fat level, stress, sleep, performance | Set deficit size and rate of loss. |
| 4 | Bodyweight or target weight, training intensity, diet preference | Set protein, fat, and carbohydrate priorities. |
| 5 | Weekly training days, equipment, injuries | Build resistance-training structure. |
| 6 | Steps, cardio ability, knee/ankle status, time cost | Allocate NEAT and cardio. |
| 7 | Weekly average weight, waist, photos, strength, hunger, sleep | Decide continue, adjust, maintain, or diet break. |

Hierarchy:

| Layer | Variable | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Calorie deficit | Determines whether fat can decrease. |
| 2 | Protein + resistance training | Determines how much muscle is retained. |
| 3 | NEAT + cardio | Stabilizes and expands total expenditure. |
| 4 | Sleep + stress + cycle management | Determines adherence and recovery. |
| 5 | Shoulder/back/glute/leg/ab training | Shapes proportion, lines, and posture. |

## Nutrition rules

Calorie deficit:

| User type | Suggested deficit | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Higher body fat, new trainee | Maintenance -500 to -750 kcal | Can lose faster, but still requires protein and lifting. |
| Ordinary body-shaping user | Maintenance -300 to -500 kcal | Best default for adherence and training stability. |
| Leaner user trying to retain muscle | Maintenance -200 to -400 kcal | Slow cut to protect performance and muscle. |
| Plateau, high stress, poor sleep | Do not automatically cut more calories | First repair records, steps, sleep, and fatigue. |

Rate:

- Most ordinary users: weekly bodyweight loss around 0.5-1%.
- Above 1% per week: watch for performance drop, uncontrolled hunger, and lean-mass loss.
- Only call it a plateau when 2-3 weeks of weekly average weight, waist, and photos do not change.

Protein:

| User type | Protein target |
|---|---|
| Ordinary training user | 1.6-2.2 g/kg bodyweight/day |
| Higher body fat | Estimate from target bodyweight or lean body mass if needed. |
| Lean, hard dieting, hard training | Up to about 2.3-3.1 g/kg lean body mass/day |
| Diet-only without lifting | Higher lean-mass loss risk; not recommended as a body-shaping strategy. |

Meal structure:

- 25-50 g protein per meal is usually more stable than concentrating all protein in one meal.
- Protein powder is just a convenient tool, not a magic fat-loss product.
- Once protein is adequate, carbohydrates and fats should support training, recovery, mood, and adherence.

Carbs, fats, fiber, water, sodium:

| Variable | Role | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrate | Training performance, glycogen, pump, mood | Prioritize around training; do not cut blindly. |
| Fat | Satiety, hormones, taste, diet stability | Do not push too low for long periods. |
| Fiber | Satiety, digestion, meal stability | Include vegetables and avoid cutting all starches. |
| Water/sodium | Bodyweight fluctuation, pump, performance | Do not panic over water weight, salt, or glycogen shifts. |

## Resistance training

Fat-loss training is not "light weight, high reps only." The main value of lifting during a cut is to tell the body to keep muscle and strength.

Rules:

- Keep compound lifts or stable heavy machine patterns in the plan.
- Preserve strength where possible instead of chasing sweat.
- Avoid frequent 1RM testing in a deficit.
- Main lifts usually keep 1-3 reps in reserve.
- Isolation work can go closer to failure when path and target tension stay clear.
- If performance drops, first check sleep, carbohydrates, total calories, and training volume before changing all key lifts.

| Variable | Hypertrophy/gain phase | Fat-loss phase |
|---|---|---|
| Main-lift intensity | Can gradually progress | Try to maintain; avoid frequent max testing |
| Volume | Can be higher | Adjust down if recovery is limited |
| Failure frequency | Can use selectively | More cautious |
| Isolation work | Can accumulate volume | Use control, reps, and tension |
| Cardio | Optional or low | Supports expenditure |
| Recovery | Usually more available | Often constrained |

Training templates:

| Template | Best fit | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| PPL body-shaping cut | Trained users with 4-6 days/week | Push, pull, legs, LISS/core, weak-point push/pull, cardio/steps, rest. |
| Full-body beginner cut | High body fat, new trainee, low skill | 3 full-body strength days + 2-4 easy cardio/step days. |
| Shape-ratio cut | Trained user focused on visual proportion | Back/rear delts, glutes/posterior chain, chest/shoulders/triceps, cardio, quads/glute med, shoulder/back/core, rest. |

## Cardio and NEAT

Cardio helps expenditure and conditioning; it does not replace lifting.

Rules:

- Avoid hard running or HIIT the day before leg day.
- 20-30 minutes low-intensity cardio after lifting is usually acceptable.
- 40-60 minutes low-intensity cardio on non-lifting days is useful when recovery allows.
- Higher bodyweight users should prioritize walking, incline walking, elliptical, or cycling over high-impact running/jumping.
- At plateau, add steps and low-intensity cardio before aggressively cutting carbohydrates.

| Cardio type | Pros | Cons | Fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| LISS | Low recovery cost, sustainable | Lower burn per minute | Most users |
| MISS | Stronger cardio stimulus | More fatigue | Users with base fitness |
| HIIT | Time efficient, intense | High recovery cost, can disrupt legs | Skilled users with strong recovery |
| Walking/incline | Simple, safe, sustainable | Time cost | Higher body fat, new users, office workers |
| Running | High expenditure | Knee/ankle impact, leg recovery cost | Lighter users with running base |

NEAT:

- Start from current step average; increase 1000-2000 steps at a time.
- Add walking commute, stairs, standing breaks every 60-90 minutes, housework, standing work, and 10-20 minute post-meal walks.
- During dieting, users often unconsciously move less. This can erase part of the deficit.

## Local shaping

Do not promise local fat loss. Local training improves muscle shape, control, posture, and proportion.

| Goal | Real requirement |
|---|---|
| Smaller waist | Overall fat loss, diet control, steps, cardio. |
| Visible abs | Lower body fat + enough abdominal muscle. |
| Lower belly | Consider fat, pelvic position, bracing, digestion/bloating. |
| Smaller-looking waist | Wider back/side delts/glutes plus waist reduction. |
| "Lines" or "tone" | Fat reduction + muscle retention + lighting/posture. |

Key shape muscles:

| Area | Value | Movements |
|---|---|---|
| Middle delts | Wider shoulders, smaller-looking waist | Dumbbell/cable/machine lateral raise |
| Rear delts | Rounder shoulders, better posture | Reverse fly, face pull, rear-delt row |
| Lats | V-taper | Pulldown, pull-up, single-arm pulldown |
| Mid/lower traps | Scapular stability | Row, Y raise, face pull |
| Upper chest | Better chest line | Incline press, incline dumbbell press |
| Glutes | Better hip/leg shape | Hip thrust, RDL, Bulgarian split squat |
| Hamstrings | Posterior-leg line | RDL, leg curl, single-leg deadlift |
| Quads | Front thigh shape | Squat, leg press, Bulgarian split squat, leg extension |
| Calves | Lower-leg shape | Standing and seated calf raise |

## Periodization

| Phase | Weeks | Goal | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Setup | 1-2 | Build records and habits | Stabilize food, steps, training before aggressive cuts. |
| Push | 3-8 | Steady loss | Protein, lifting, cardio, steps. |
| Fatigue management | 9-12 | Manage plateau and fatigue | Adjust calories, cardio, and lifting volume. |
| Maintenance | 1-4 | Lock in result | Return to maintenance, restore performance, prevent rebound. |

Refeed and diet breaks:

| Method | Use |
|---|---|
| Refeed/high-carb day | 1-2 days near maintenance, mainly higher carbs; use when training/glycogen/psychological pressure need relief. |
| Diet break | 7-14 days at maintenance after long dieting, plateau, or high fatigue. |
| Maintenance week | Hold result and restore performance. |
| Cheat meal | Not preferred as an unplanned binge; can erase deficit. |

## Plateau handling

Not a plateau:

- 3 days with no bodyweight change.
- One salty meal causing 1 kg increase.
- Leg-day soreness/water retention.
- Menstrual-cycle fluctuation.
- Incomplete dietary records.

Likely plateau:

- 2-3 weeks of unchanged weekly average weight, waist, and photos.

Adjustment order:

1. Check records: oils, sauces, snacks, drinks, and weekends.
2. Use weekly average bodyweight, not single-day scale weight.
3. Add 1000-3000 daily steps.
4. Add 1-2 low-intensity cardio sessions, 25-40 minutes each.
5. Reduce calories by only 100-200 kcal/day if adherence is confirmed.
6. Reduce training fatigue if strength is crashing.
7. Use diet break when fatigue, hunger, and sleep problems accumulate.

## User types

| User type | Training | Cardio/steps | Diet | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Higher body fat/new trainee | 3 full-body strength days | 2-4 walks/elliptical, steps toward 8000-10000 | Moderate deficit, high protein/fiber | Build habit; avoid HIIT, zero-carb, daily hard training. |
| Office worker shaping | 3-5 strength days | After lifting 20-30 min or non-training 40 min | 300-500 kcal deficit | Sleep, steps, protein, dinner control, weekend adherence. |
| Trained but higher fat | Keep PPL or upper/lower | 2-4 sessions/week | High protein, carbs around training | Maintain strength; avoid turning lifting into calorie-burning circuits. |
| Already lean, refining | High-quality lifting with fatigue control | Low-impact | Small deficit and slow loss | Sleep, stress, performance, muscle retention. |

## Supplements

| Supplement | Role | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Whey | Helps meet protein | Useful, not required. |
| Creatine | Supports strength/performance | Recommended for many lifters. |
| Caffeine | Performance and focus | Optional. |
| Fish oil | Useful if diet lacks omega-3 | Optional. |
| Multivitamin | Covers gaps in poor diets | Optional. |
| L-carnitine | Limited fat-loss effect | Not core. |
| Fat burners | Often caffeine/stimulants | Do not rely on them. |
| Meal replacement | Can control calories | Tool, not a long-term skill replacement. |

## Output requirements

When using this reference, include:

- Primary target: fat loss, recomposition, body shaping, posture, or contest-style cut.
- Known data versus assumptions: weight trend, waist, photos, performance, diet, steps, sleep.
- Resistance training anchor: split, key lifts, weekly volume, RPE/RIR, and what to preserve.
- Cardio and NEAT plan: step target changes, cardio type, duration, and timing around lifting.
- Nutrition direction: deficit size, protein target logic, carbs/fats/fiber principles; avoid extreme claims.
- Plateau rule: what must be true before adjusting, and what to adjust first.
- Tracking: 7-day average weight, waist, photos, strength, hunger, sleep, steps, mood, and adherence.
