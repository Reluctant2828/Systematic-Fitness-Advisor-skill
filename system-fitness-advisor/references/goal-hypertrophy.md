# Goal Module: Hypertrophy

Use this module when the user's primary goal is 增肌, 肌肉量, 变壮, 围度提升, or improving muscle size while keeping recovery manageable.

When the user asks how to arrange weekly training, choose between 二分化, 三分化, 四分化, 五分化, PPL, upper/lower, or other hypertrophy splits by reading `hypertrophy-splits.md`. When the request asks how to execute a specific split, read the matching reference: `split-two-division.md`, `ppl-practical.md`, `split-four-division.md`, or `split-five-division.md`.

## Primary objective

Increase target-muscle stimulus across recoverable weekly volume, sufficient frequency, stable technique, and progressive overload.

## Inputs to prioritize

- Target muscles and weak areas.
- Current weekly hard sets per muscle.
- Recent 2-6 week performance trend.
- Exercise execution quality and mind-muscle connection.
- Recovery signals: soreness duration, sleep, motivation, joint stress.
- Available equipment and load jumps.

## Programming defaults

- Weekly volume: start around 8-12 hard sets per muscle when data is sparse; use 10-20 for trained muscles when recovery supports it.
- Frequency: 2-3 exposures per muscle per week for most muscles.
- Reps: 6-15 for most compounds; 10-30 for isolations.
- Intensity: most working sets at 0-3 RIR; avoid frequent failure on heavy compounds.
- Rest: 2-4 minutes for compounds, 1-2 minutes for isolations.
- Exercise mix: include stable high-load work plus target-biased isolation or machine/cable work.

## Progression

Use double progression by default:

1. Keep load stable while adding reps within the target range.
2. When all sets reach the top of the range at the target RIR, increase load by the smallest practical jump.
3. If load jumps are too large, progress with reps, pauses, slower eccentrics, density, or set quality.

## Adjustment rules

- Add 1-2 hard sets per week to a target muscle only when performance and recovery are stable.
- Reduce 20-40% volume for one week if performance drops across 2+ sessions or joint stress rises.
- Rotate exercises when the target muscle is no longer well-loaded, the movement becomes stale, or pain appears.
- Do not add volume to every muscle at once; prioritize one or two muscles.

## Output emphasis

Report target muscle, weekly set target, exercise slots, rep ranges, RIR, progression rule, and what to monitor next.
