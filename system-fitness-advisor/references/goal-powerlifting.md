# Goal Module: Powerlifting

Use this module when the user's primary goal is 力量举, SBD, 深蹲卧推硬拉, one-rep max, peaking, meet preparation, or improving competition-style squat, bench press, and deadlift. Read `powerlifting-advanced.md` when the request involves SBD technique, sticking points, top singles, back-off sets, DUP, 12-week cycles, attempt selection, peaking, or mixed powerlifting + hypertrophy.

## Primary objective

Improve squat, bench, and deadlift performance through skill practice, planned intensity exposure, fatigue management, and specific accessory work.

## Inputs to prioritize

- Current squat, bench, deadlift numbers and recent top sets.
- Estimated 1RM trend and RPE accuracy.
- Competition date or testing timeline.
- Technical weak points by lift.
- Recovery, bodyweight trend, and injury constraints.
- Equipment: rack, bench, barbell, plates, belt, straps, sleeves, specialty bars.

Read `powerlifting-advanced.md` when the request involves:

- 深蹲, 卧推, 硬拉 technique, rules, or sticking points.
- 1RM, e1RM, test day, meet prep, peaking, taper, opener/second/third attempts.
- Top single @RPE, back-off sets, percentage ranges, DUP, 12-week cycle.
- Assistance selection for squat/bench/deadlift weak points.
- Combining powerlifting with hypertrophy or body-part specialization.

## Programming defaults

- Competition lifts should appear regularly unless pain or equipment prevents them.
- Main lift work: mostly 1-6 reps, RPE 6-9.
- Secondary variations: 3-8 reps for weak points and technical practice.
- Accessories: 6-15+ reps for muscle, balance, and injury resilience.
- Bench usually tolerates higher frequency than deadlift.
- Deadlift fatigue is expensive; dose hard pulls carefully.

## Block structure

- Accumulation: more volume, technical consistency, hypertrophy support.
- Strength: heavier specific work, reduced accessory fatigue.
- Peaking: higher specificity, lower volume, planned singles.
- Deload/test: reduce fatigue before max testing or meet week.

## Progression

- Use top set plus back-off sets, percentage ranges, or RPE-based load selection.
- Track e1RM trends, but do not chase e1RM at the cost of standards or technique.
- Increase load only when bar path, depth/touch/lockout, and RPE are credible.

## Adjustment rules

- If a lift stalls, diagnose technical issue, volume dose, intensity exposure, and recovery before adding random accessories.
- If deadlift fatigue spills into squat or life recovery, reduce deadlift hard sets first.
- If bench stalls but recovery is good, add frequency or targeted triceps/chest/upper-back work.
- If meet prep is near, avoid novelty and prioritize specificity.

## Output emphasis

Report weekly SBD structure, intensity/RPE targets, variation purpose, accessory rationale, deload/test timing, and metrics to track.
