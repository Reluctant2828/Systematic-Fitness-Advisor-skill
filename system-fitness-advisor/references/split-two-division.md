# Two-Day Split Advanced Knowledge Base

Use this reference with `goal-hypertrophy.md` and `hypertrophy-splits.md` when the user asks about 二分化, Upper/Lower, 上肢/下肢, 上肢 A/B, 下肢 A/B, 水平推拉, 膝主导, 垂直推拉, 髋主导, 4 天二分化, 3 天滚动二分化, or adding a weak-point day to an upper/lower split.

## Core definition

二分化 is not simply "upper body / lower body." It splits whole-body training into two stable modules, then uses A/B emphases to make weekly stimulus complete:

- Upper A: horizontal push/pull, bench and row emphasis.
- Lower A: knee-dominant, quads, squat pattern, foot/ankle and core support.
- Upper B: vertical push/pull, lats, shoulder development, rear delts.
- Lower B: hip-dominant, posterior chain, glutes, hamstrings, pelvic stability.

The standard 4-day version is Upper A / Lower A / Upper B / Lower B. Its advantage is distributing weekly volume across about two exposures per muscle, improving set quality, recovery stability, and long-term progression.

## Frequency selection

| Weekly frequency | Recommendation | Fit |
|---|---|---|
| 2 days | Prefer full-body or a simplified upper/lower with clear limitations | Strict upper/lower gives each muscle too little frequency for most hypertrophy goals. |
| 3 days | Rolling upper/lower: upper/lower/upper, then lower/upper/lower next week | Good when time is limited but frequency should be maintained. |
| 4 days | Upper A / Lower A / Upper B / Lower B | Most stable and recommended default. |
| 5 days | Four-day upper/lower plus one weak-point day | Use only when recovery is good and the weak point is clear. |

## Standard 4-day week

| Day | Content | Focus | Recovery logic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Upper A | Horizontal push/pull: bench, row, chest/back thickness | Upper body gets 48-72 hours before the next upper exposure. |
| Day 2 | Lower A | Knee-dominant: squat, leg press, quads | Local fatigue does not overlap with upper day. |
| Day 3 | Rest | Walking, mobility, easy activity | Reduces systemic fatigue. |
| Day 4 | Upper B | Vertical push/pull: pulldown/pull-up, press, shoulder/back | Complements Upper A angles. |
| Day 5 | Lower B | Hip-dominant: RDL, hip thrust, posterior chain | Complements Lower A. |
| Day 6 | Rest or easy cardio | Low-fatigue activity or small weak-point work | Do not steal next week's main-lift performance. |
| Day 7 | Rest | Sleep, food, recovery | Prepare next cycle. |

## Upper A: horizontal push/pull

Goal: chest/back thickness, bench strength, row strength, and scapular stability. Upper A is not a chest day; it pairs horizontal push and horizontal pull.

| Movement | Sets x reps | RIR | Execution focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barbell or dumbbell bench press | 3-4 x 4-8 | 1-3 | Scapula retracted/depressed, open chest, stable unrack, controlled elbow angle. |
| Barbell row or seated row | 3-4 x 6-10 | 1-2 | Elbow drives back, scapula retracts, avoid shrugging and low-back compensation. |
| Incline dumbbell press | 2-3 x 8-12 | 1-2 | Upper-chest stretch control; do not chase load at the cost of shoulder position. |
| Pulldown or pull-up | 3 x 8-12 | 1-2 | Scapula depresses first; keep lat tension. |
| Lateral raise | 3-5 x 12-20 | 0-2 | Progress with control, reps, and target-muscle tension before load. |
| Rope pressdown | 2-3 x 10-15 | 0-1 | Elbow fixed, full lockout without body English. |
| Dumbbell curl | 2-3 x 10-15 | 0-1 | No swinging; controlled eccentric. |

Rules:

- Bench develops push capability and screens scapular stability; it is not pure chest isolation.
- Rowing must balance pressing. Long-term push dominance raises anterior shoulder stress and limits back development.
- Back volume should usually be at least equal to chest volume.

## Lower A: knee-dominant/quads

Goal: quads, squat pattern, foot/ankle stability, knee tracking, and core support. Lower A is not "all leg movements"; it develops knee-dominant capability.

| Movement | Sets x reps | RIR | Execution focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Squat or hack squat | 3-5 x 4-8 | 2-3 | Stable feet, knees track with toes, trunk tension stays high. |
| Leg press | 3-4 x 8-12 | 1-2 | Control bottom position; no bouncing or partial-range load chasing. |
| Bulgarian split squat | 2-3 x 8-12/side | 1-2 | Pelvis stable; screen left-right difference. |
| Leg extension | 2-4 x 12-20 | 0-1 | Quad peak contraction without swinging. |
| Leg curl | 2-3 x 10-15 | 1 | Base hamstring stimulus while preserving Lower B recovery. |
| Calf raise | 3-5 x 8-15 | 1 | Bottom stretch, top pause, full range. |
| Anti-extension core | 2-4 sets | Technical | Dead bug, plank, or crunch variation with control. |

Rules:

- Squat, hack squat, and leg press prioritize quads and support ability.
- Do not take heavy squat or leg press to failure frequently.
- Single-leg movements are both hypertrophy work and stability assessment.

## Upper B: vertical push/pull

Goal: lat width, shoulder shape, overhead pressing, rear delts, and scapular stability. Upper B changes the stimulus direction rather than repeating Upper A.

| Movement | Sets x reps | RIR | Execution focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pull-up or pulldown | 3-4 x 6-10 | 1-2 | Scapula depresses before elbows pull; keep lat tension. |
| Seated or dumbbell overhead press | 3-4 x 5-10 | 1-3 | Core stable; avoid shrugging or lumbar compensation. |
| Incline press or machine chest press | 2-3 x 8-12 | 1-2 | Add chest volume without stealing main-lift recovery. |
| Chest-supported row or one-arm row | 3 x 8-12 | 1-2 | Reduce low-back compensation and increase back tension. |
| Rear-delt fly or face pull | 3-4 x 12-20 | 0-1 | Rear delts, mid/lower traps, and scapular stability are real work, not warm-up filler. |
| Overhead triceps extension | 2-3 x 10-15 | 0-1 | Long-head triceps stretch. |
| Incline curl or hammer curl | 2-3 x 10-15 | 0-1 | Biceps long head and brachialis supplement. |

Rules:

- Back training prioritizes scapular depression and sustained lat tension.
- Shoulder training cannot rely on pressing only; side and rear delts need controlled high-quality work.

## Lower B: hip-dominant/posterior chain

Goal: glutes, hamstrings, hip hinge, pelvic stability, and posterior-chain strength. Lower B's core is hip hinge, not another quad day.

| Movement | Sets x reps | RIR | Execution focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Romanian deadlift | 3-4 x 5-10 | 2 | Hips fold back, hamstrings lengthen, spine stays neutral. |
| Hip thrust or glute bridge | 3-4 x 6-12 | 1-2 | Posterior pelvic tilt at top; active glute contraction. |
| Front squat or moderate leg press | 2-3 x 8-12 | 2 | Keep some quad work without stealing posterior-chain focus. |
| Seated or prone leg curl | 3-4 x 10-15 | 0-1 | Hamstring knee-flexion function and controlled eccentric. |
| Hip abduction | 2-4 x 12-25 | 0-1 | Glute med and hip stability. |
| Calf raise | 3-5 x 10-20 | 1 | Slow eccentric and full range. |
| Anti-rotation core | 2-4 sets | Technical | Pallof press, side plank, or similar. |

Rules:

- Romanian deadlift is not conventional deadlift load chasing; it builds hip hinge, posterior tension, and hamstring length control.
- RDL and hip thrust are not redundant: RDL emphasizes lengthened posterior chain; hip thrust emphasizes peak glute contraction.
- Leg curls should stay because hamstrings perform both hip extension and knee flexion.

## Training variables

Weekly volume:

| Level | Effective sets per muscle/week | Use |
|---|---|---|
| New | 6-10 | Build movement patterns; avoid early volume overload. |
| Early-intermediate | 8-14 | Stable hypertrophy range for most users. |
| Intermediate | 10-18 | Adjust by recovery and weak points. |
| Specialization block | 14-22 | Only for clear weak points with recovery monitoring. |

Intensity:

| Movement type | Recommended effort | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Squat, bench, RDL, overhead press | RIR 1-3 | Main lifts prioritize quality and long-term progression, not failure. |
| Row, pulldown, leg press, hip thrust | RIR 1-2 | Can approach failure while keeping path stable. |
| Lateral raise, fly, curl, pressdown, leg extension | RIR 0-2 | Can get closer to failure; use pump and control. |
| High-risk compounds | Avoid frequent RIR 0 | Fatigue and technique breakdown risk is high. |

Rest:

| Movement type | Rest |
|---|---|
| Heavy compound | 2-4 min |
| Moderate compound | 90 sec-3 min |
| Isolation | 45-120 sec |
| Pump/metabolic finishers | 30-90 sec |

Rep ranges:

- Max strength/main intensity: 3-6 reps for squat, bench, hinge, press variants.
- Main hypertrophy range: 6-12 reps for presses, rows, leg press, pull-ups, hip thrust.
- Volume range: 10-20 reps for flyes, lateral raises, leg curls/extensions, curls.
- Pump/metabolic range: 15-30 reps for small muscles, isolations, and machines.

## Progression and cycle

Use double progression:

| Current result | Next strategy |
|---|---|
| 30 kg: 10, 9, 8 | Keep load and increase total reps. |
| 30 kg: 12, 11, 10 | Keep load and complete remaining reps. |
| 30 kg: 12, 12, 12 | Add a small valid load next time. |
| 32.5 kg: 9, 8, 7 | Normal drop after load increase; rebuild reps. |

Four-week microcycle plus deload:

| Week | Strategy |
|---|---|
| 1 | RIR about 3; technique and volume adaptation. |
| 2 | RIR about 2; small load or rep increases. |
| 3 | RIR about 1; main stimulus week. |
| 4 | Some isolation work RIR 0-1; main lifts do not force failure. |
| 5 | Deload: sets cut about half, load down 10-15%. |

## Weak-point insertion

| Weak point | Adjustment | Caution |
|---|---|---|
| Chest | Upper A bench first; Upper B adds incline or machine press. | Maintain back volume; avoid excessive triceps fatigue. |
| Back | Upper A row first; Upper B pull-up/pulldown first; back 14-18 sets/week. | Chest drops to maintenance; focus scapular control. |
| Shoulders | Lateral raises on both upper days; add rear-delt fly or face pull on Upper B. | Pressing is not complete shoulder training. |
| Glutes/posterior chain | Lower B RDL first; keep hip thrust; leg curl 6-8 sets/week. | Reduce Lower A quad work if recovery conflicts. |
| Quads | Lower A squat/hack first; increase leg press and extension volume. | Keep posterior chain at maintenance. |

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Upper day becomes chest day plus token back work | Keep push/pull balanced; back volume usually no lower than chest. |
| Lower day has only squat and leg press | Cover knee extension, hip extension, knee flexion, ankle extension, and core. |
| Every movement uses 8-12 reps | Use lower reps for main lifts and higher reps for isolations. |
| Every set goes to failure | Main lifts keep 1-3 RIR; isolations can approach failure. |
| Copying high-level low-volume training | Build execution control and target-muscle feel first. |
| Only tracking load | Track load, reps, RIR, range, pain, and recovery. |

## Output requirements

When using this reference, include:

- Whether the user should run 3-day rolling, 4-day standard, or 5-day weak-point upper/lower.
- The four emphasis days if using standard 二分化: Upper A, Lower A, Upper B, Lower B.
- Exercise slots, sets, reps, RIR, rest, and progression.
- Push/pull balance for upper days and knee/hip balance for lower days.
- Weak-point adjustments only when recovery allows, with strong muscles reduced to maintenance if needed.
