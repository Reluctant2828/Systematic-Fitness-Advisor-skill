# Five-Day Split Advanced Knowledge Base

Use this reference with `goal-hypertrophy.md` and `hypertrophy-splits.md` when the user asks about 五分化, 5-day split, chest/back/legs/shoulders/arms, 胸日, 背日, 腿日, 肩日, 手臂日, 弱项补充日, 主训练日 + 二次刺激, or whether a user should run a five-day hypertrophy/body-shaping split.

## Fit and boundaries

五分化 is best for users who can train 5 days consistently, have basic movement competency, and primarily want hypertrophy, body shaping, or weak-point specialization.

Do not default to 五分化 when the user is a new trainee with unstable technique, has poor recovery or long-term poor sleep, cannot reliably train 5 days, or wants a short-term strength peak.

If the user only asks for "one body part per day" without understanding volume, intensity, frequency, and recovery, explain that advanced 五分化 is a training organization system, not just a body-part list.

## Core definition

Advanced 五分化 = 5 training days + enough weekly volume per muscle + secondary stimulus for priority muscles + main lifts with reps in reserve + high-quality isolation work + fatigue management.

It is not simply chest, back, legs, shoulders, and arms each once per week. The fifth day is flexible: arms if arms are weak, Legs B if legs are weak, or a weak-point/secondary-stimulus day for chest, back, shoulders, or glutes.

## Default structure

| Day | Structure | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Chest + light/moderate triceps | Chest main stimulus: press + fly/pec deck; do not destroy triceps. |
| Day 2 | Back + light/moderate biceps | Back main stimulus: vertical pull + horizontal row; moderate biceps. |
| Day 3 | Legs A | Quads, knee-dominant work, base lower-body volume; small posterior-chain dose allowed. |
| Day 4 | Shoulders + rear delts | Side and rear delts are the core; pressing is useful but not excessive. |
| Day 5 | Arms + weak-point supplement or Legs B | Insert chest, back, shoulders, legs, or arms based on weak point. |
| Day 6 | Rest or low-intensity cardio | Recovery, mobility, walking. |
| Day 7 | Rest | Full recovery or low-stress activity. |

Common templates:

| Template | Weekly structure | Fit |
|---|---|---|
| Standard hypertrophy 五分化 | Chest+triceps / Back+biceps / Legs A / Shoulders+rear delts / Arms+chest-back weak point | Most intermediate hypertrophy users. |
| Leg weak-point 五分化 | Chest+triceps / Back+biceps / Legs A / Shoulders+rear delts / Legs B posterior-chain/glutes/calves | Users with lagging legs or glutes. |
| Shoulder-arm weak-point 五分化 | Chest+small side delts / Back+rear delts / Legs / Shoulder specialization / Arms+side-delt supplement | Users lacking shoulder width, rear delts, or arm size. |

## Weak-point insertion

| Weak point | Insertion | Caution |
|---|---|---|
| Chest | Add 2-4 sets incline press or fly/pec deck on arm day; keep shoulder day front-delt fatigue controlled. | Prioritize upper chest, stretch, and contraction. |
| Back | Add 2-5 sets pulldown or row on arm day; reduce sloppy heavy work on back day. | Scapular control and lat tension first. |
| Shoulder width | Add 3-5 sets lateral raise on chest or arm day. | High reps, slow eccentric, pauses; no swinging. |
| Rear delts | Add rear-delt work on both back and shoulder days. | Reverse fly/face pull should not become trap shrugging. |
| Legs | Make Day 5 Legs B. | Separate quad focus from posterior-chain/glute focus. |
| Arms | Reduce arm fatigue on chest/back days and raise direct arm volume on arm day. | Train biceps and triceps by head/function. |

## Chest day

Chest day uses pressing to build tension and capability, then fly/pec deck/cable work to fill stretch and contraction stimulus.

| Type | Movements | Range | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main press | Barbell bench, incline barbell press | 3-4 x 5-8 | RIR 1-3; stable technique first. |
| Secondary press | Dumbbell press, machine press, Smith press | 2-4 x 8-12 | Add volume without shoulder dumping forward. |
| Stretch stimulus | Dumbbell fly, cable fly | 2-4 x 10-20 | Slow eccentric and clear chest stretch. |
| Contraction stimulus | Pec deck, cable crossover | 2-4 x 12-20 | Peak contraction pause. |
| Triceps assistant | Rope pressdown, dips, close-grip press | 4-8 sets/week | Adjust by shoulder/arm-day recovery. |

Rules:

- Bench or incline press screens the push chain: scapular retraction/depression, thoracic extension, leg drive, and elbow path.
- Incline angle should not be so high that front delts take over.
- Fly and cable movements do not chase max load; use trajectory, stretch, and squeeze.

## Back day

| Type | Movements | Purpose | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical pull | Pull-up, pulldown | Lat width | Scapular depression; elbow down/toward hip. |
| Horizontal row | Chest-supported row, seated row, T-bar row | Upper-back thickness | Stable torso; no shrugging. |
| Unilateral pull | One-arm cable pulldown, one-arm row | Correct asymmetry | Stable path, pull near hip. |
| Rear delt/scapula | Reverse fly, face pull | Rear delts and scapular stability | Light load and control. |
| Posterior-chain assistant | RDL, back extension | Hip extension and low-back/posterior capacity | Do not load-chase in fatigue phases. |

Rules:

- Lats: scapula depresses first, then elbow travels toward the hip; avoid biceps-dominant pulling.
- Upper back: controlled scapular retraction and appropriate elbow flare.
- Range of motion serves target tension; a bigger range is not automatically better.
- Heavy back work is allowed only when target loading is clear. Ordinary trainees should not copy advanced low-volume high-intensity back work.

## Legs day

| Type | Movements | Target | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Knee-dominant | Squat, hack squat, leg press | Quads | Stable knee path and foot arch. |
| Hip-dominant | RDL, hip thrust | Glutes/hamstrings | Clear hinge and sustained posterior tension. |
| Unilateral | Bulgarian split squat, lunge | Hip/knee/ankle stability | Control center of mass; no knee collapse. |
| Isolation | Leg extension, leg curl | Local volume | Peak contraction and slow eccentric. |
| Calves | Standing calf raise, seated calf raise | Gastrocnemius/soleus | Stretch bottom, contract top. |

Rules:

- Leg day is not just squat day; cover quads, posterior chain, glutes, calves, and unilateral stability.
- When fatigue is high, technique is unstable, or the user is new, do not use conventional deadlift as blind heavy main work.

## Shoulder day

| Type | Movements | Range | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Press | Seated press, Smith press | 2-4 x 5-10 | Build shoulder strength without excessive front-delt volume. |
| Side delts | Dumbbell, cable, or machine lateral raise | 6-12 sets/week | Stable path, no shrugging or swinging. |
| Rear delts | Reverse fly, face pull | 6-10 sets/week | Light load, control, peak pause. |
| Scapular stability | Y raise, lower-trap work | 2-6 sets/week | Support scapular rhythm. |

Rules:

- Chest day already hits front delts heavily, so shoulder day should prioritize side delts and rear delts.
- Shoulder width comes mostly from side delts; posture and shoulder completeness rely heavily on rear delts/scapular control.
- Lateral raises and rear-delt flyes progress by reps, control, pauses, and pump before load.

## Arms/weak-point day

| Area | Movements | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Biceps long head | Incline dumbbell curl | Shoulder-extended curl, lengthened work. |
| Biceps short head | Preacher curl, machine curl | Stable curl with reduced swinging. |
| Brachialis | Hammer curl | Neutral grip, arm thickness. |
| Triceps long head | Overhead extension, cable overhead extension | Shoulder-flexed long-head stimulus. |
| Triceps lateral head | Rope pressdown | Stable elbow extension and peak contraction. |
| Triceps compound | Close-grip bench, dips | Full elbow-extension capability, but higher fatigue. |

Rules:

- Arm day is not random curls and pressdowns. Cover elbow flexion/extension functions and head-specific positions.
- Arm isolations can approach failure if elbows feel good and the path is stable.
- Arm day can also carry weak-point secondary stimulus for chest, back, shoulders, or legs.

## Weekly volume and frequency

Effective sets do not include warm-up sets. Effective sets require standard execution, clear target loading, and proximity to failure without requiring every set to fail.

| Muscle | Weekly sets | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chest | 10-18 | Chest day main volume plus small shoulder/arm-day supplement. |
| Back | 12-22 | Cover both vertical pulls and horizontal rows. |
| Quads | 10-18 | Legs A main; Legs B can supplement if needed. |
| Hamstrings/glutes | 8-16 | RDL, leg curl, hip thrust. |
| Side delts | 10-20 | Can be distributed across shoulder and arm/chest days. |
| Rear delts | 8-16 | Back and shoulder days both can include them. |
| Biceps | 6-14 | Back-day indirect plus arm-day direct work. |
| Triceps | 6-14 | Chest-day indirect plus arm-day direct work. |
| Calves | 8-18 | Often benefits from higher frequency. |
| Abs | 6-12 | Adjust by recovery and posture/body-shaping goals. |

Priority frequency:

| Muscle | Main stimulus | Secondary stimulus |
|---|---|---|
| Chest | Chest day 9-12 sets | 2-5 sets fly or incline press on arm/shoulder day. |
| Back | Back day 10-15 sets | 2-5 sets pulldown or row on arm day. |
| Legs | Legs A quad-focused | Day 5 can become Legs B for posterior chain/glutes/calves. |
| Shoulders | Shoulder day side/rear focus | Chest day already hits front delts; back day can add rear delts. |
| Arms | Indirect on chest/back days | Direct arm day. |

## Intensity, rest, and progression

| Movement type | Intensity | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Main compounds | RIR 1-3 | Bench, squat, row, RDL should not fail every set. |
| Machine compounds | RIR 0-2 | More stable; can approach failure. |
| Isolation | RIR 0-2 | Flyes, raises, curls, pressdowns can push hard on final sets. |
| Pump finishers | Near failure | No form breakdown or joint pain. |
| Unstable technique | Avoid failure | Stabilize the path before increasing effort. |

| Movement type | Rest | Reps |
|---|---|---|
| Bench, squat, RDL, row | 2-4 min | 5-8 |
| Machine press, pulldown, leg press | 90-180 sec | 8-15 |
| Lateral raise, fly, curl, pressdown | 60-120 sec | 10-20 |
| Side/rear delts, calves | 45-120 sec | 12-25 |
| Drop set, 10+5, pump finisher | 45-90 sec | 15-30 |

Double progression:

| Movement | Target | Add load when | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bench/incline press | 3 x 6-10 | Around 10/10/9-10 with stable form | Scapula does not lose position. |
| Machine press/row | 3 x 8-12 | Around 12/12/11-12 with stable path | Target muscle remains clear. |
| Lateral raise/fly | 3-4 x 12-20 | Reps, pauses, and eccentric improve first | No shrugging or swinging. |
| Curl/pressdown | 2-4 x 10-20 | Control and peak contraction improve | Protect elbows. |

## Cycle and deload

| Stage | Goal | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1-2 | Find movement and tension, build baseline | RIR 2-3, avoid excessive failure, record load and execution. |
| Weeks 3-5 | Add reps or load, gradually increase volume | RIR 1-2, progress priority movements. |
| Weeks 6-7 | Isolations approach failure; volume peaks | Use drop sets or 10+5 selectively. |
| Week 8 | Deload or active recovery | Sets down 30-50%, load down 5-15%, keep 3-5 RIR. |

Deload when:

- The same movement drops for 2-3 sessions.
- Sleep, appetite, or training drive worsens.
- Shoulder, elbow, knee, or low-back discomfort increases.
- Pump and target-muscle feel fall while compensation rises.
- Weak-point volume creates systemic fatigue.

## Decision rules

Use the order: training age -> time availability -> equipment -> goal -> weak point -> recovery.

- If the user cannot consistently train 5 days, do not directly recommend 五分化; prefer 三分化, 四分化, or upper/lower.
- If the user is new, treat 五分化 as knowledge, not the first-choice plan. Build squat, bench, row, hip hinge, press, and pulldown ability first.
- If recovery is poor, reduce weekly sets and failure frequency before adding training days or intensity techniques.
- If chest is weak, add chest secondary stimulus on Day 5; if legs are weak, make Day 5 Legs B; if shoulders/arms are weak, separate shoulder and arm emphasis.
- If joints are uncomfortable, prefer machines, cables, dumbbells, and controllable paths; do not force heavy barbell work.
- If the goal is body shaping and size, prioritize volume, angles, and isolation quality. If the goal is strength, increase main-lift share and reduce isolation clutter.

## Substitution rules

| Problem | Substitute | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Barbell bench discomfort | Dumbbell press, machine press, Smith press | Keep chest press pattern while reducing shoulder stress. |
| Unstable squat | Hack squat, leg press, goblet squat | Build knee-dominant capacity first. |
| Conventional deadlift fatigue too high | RDL, hip thrust, back extension | Keep posterior-chain training with lower systemic cost. |
| Cannot do pull-ups | Pulldown, assisted pull-up, single-arm pulldown | Build lat pattern first. |
| Lateral raise compensation | Cable, machine, or seated lateral raise | Reduce cheating and improve side-delt tension. |
| Elbow discomfort | Cable curl/pressdown, neutral grip, less close-grip pressing | Reduce joint stress. |

## Output requirements

When using this reference, include:

- Whether 五分化 is appropriate; if not, explain the better split.
- The five-day structure and whether Day 5 is arms, weak point, or Legs B.
- Main stimulus and secondary stimulus for priority muscles.
- Exercise slots with sets, reps, RIR, rest, and progression.
- Deload triggers and what to reduce first when recovery falls.
