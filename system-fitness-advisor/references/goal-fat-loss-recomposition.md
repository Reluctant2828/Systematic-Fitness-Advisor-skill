# Goal Module: Fat Loss And Recomposition

Use this module when the user's primary goal is 减脂, 塑形, 体脂下降, 变瘦, 线条, or recomposition while preserving muscle and training performance. Read `fat-loss-recomposition-advanced.md` when the user asks about calorie deficit, cardio, NEAT, plateau, body-shaping proportions, local shaping, diet breaks, or how to adjust training while dieting.

## Primary objective

Preserve or improve strength and muscle stimulus while bodyweight, waist, photos, or other body-composition indicators trend in the desired direction.

## Inputs to prioritize

- Current weight trend, waist/measurements, photos, or adherence signals.
- Training performance trend on key lifts.
- Weekly schedule, sleep, stress, steps, and cardio.
- Current lifting volume and fatigue.
- Diet context only when provided; do not invent precise intake.

Read `fat-loss-recomposition-advanced.md` when the request involves:

- 热量缺口, 蛋白质, 碳水, 脂肪, 膳食纤维, 水钠.
- 有氧, LISS, HIIT, 快走, 爬坡, NEAT, 步数.
- 腰腹线条, 肩背塑形, 臀腿塑形, 局部减脂误区.
- 平台期, refeed, diet break, 维持热量周.
- 减脂期怎么保力量, 保肌肉, or avoid turning lifting into calorie-burning circuits.

## Programming defaults

- Keep resistance training as the anchor.
- Weekly volume: usually 8-14 hard sets per major muscle when recovery is limited.
- Frequency: 2 exposures per major muscle is often enough; 3 can work if sessions are short and recoverable.
- Reps: keep compounds and machines in productive hypertrophy/strength ranges; avoid turning every set into metabolic fatigue.
- Conditioning: add steps or low-impact cardio gradually.
- Deficit caution: avoid extreme deficit recommendations or medical claims.

## Progression

- First win: maintain load, reps, and technique while body metrics improve.
- Second win: small rep or load increases on key lifts.
- If performance drops sharply, reduce cardio, reduce lifting volume slightly, or improve recovery before adding more work.

## Adjustment rules

- If weight/waist does not trend down and adherence is good, add small activity or nutrition adjustments rather than cutting training quality.
- If strength drops for 2+ sessions, reduce accessory volume or conditioning before changing key lifts.
- Use machines/cables when fatigue is high and technique consistency matters.
- Keep enough protein and recovery context general unless user provides nutrition details.

## Output emphasis

Report the training plan, performance-preservation targets, activity/cardio suggestions, fatigue control, and body-composition metrics to track.
